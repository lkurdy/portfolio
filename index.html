Bomberman - 2024

Unreal Engine 5 always fascinated me, when I finally built my first PC in 2023 I immediately started learning to use it. This little project is just a demonstration of what I learnt on my own.

Just like the original Bomberman, you place a bomb, it explodes in a plus shape and destroys the crates that it hits. If it hits a player, he dies, if it hits another bomb it detonates in a chain reaction. There are stat upgrades that spawn randomly when a crate is destroyed.

I made the character's movement in C++, the rest in Blueprints. I also made the explosion projectile and the upgrades with the Niagara system.

ft_transcendence - 2023/2024

Ft_transcendence, our final 42 project. It's a dynamic single-page app featuring a pong game, chat functionality, online game mode, and an array of features. While video demonstrations may be limited, I invite you to explore the repository or dive directly into some of my contributions.

Three of us worked on the project, none of us had any experience in web development beforehand. I handled the backend game server, implementing game mechanics, and handling sockets using Typescript with NestJS and React. Working as a team was a challenge, but also a great skill that we developed.

This project put everything we've learnt into practice. We are very proud of the journey and the result.

Cub3D - 2022

Cub3D was also a 42 project made using the library MinilibX in C.

In a team of two, we recreated the graphical environment of Wolfenstein 3D, developed by id Software. I learned and implemented the raycasting technique, while my partner worked on analysing the game's map, including textures, the player's starting position, and orientation.

Raycasting allowed me to turn a 2D map, like the one above, into a 3D perspective. We can move around the map smoothly and rotate the camera to view the walls properly from different angles.
